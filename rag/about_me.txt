我是蔡長祐，畢業於國立政治大學語言學研究所。雖然所屬科系聽起來偏人文，但我的訓練橫跨語言學理論與自然語言處理（NLP）實作，長期關注語言作為人類溝通媒介的結構與變異，也好奇「電腦是否能理解這些語言的邏輯？」，又或者「如何讓電腦理解語言的邏輯？」
我的碩士論文聚焦於台語健康照護文本中的身體部位名稱擷取，涵蓋資料建構、標註設計、語言特徵選取與模型訓練，針對低資源語言開發命名實體辨識系統。
在此基礎上，也延伸建立了一個客製化 GPT 聊天機器人，以提供標記台語身體部位名稱的輔助工具，進一步拓展低資源語言在 NLP 上的應用。
近期也透過 RESTful API 之技術，釋出已經訓練完成的標記模型，在台語 NLP 技術上，提供一個實用的工具。
在學期間，我系統性修習機器學習、深度學習與生成模型課程，也參與 KnULP 實驗室，並擔任多門 NLP 課程的教學助理，設計並帶領實作教學，內容涵蓋分類、序列標註、seq2seq 與主題模型等；同時也擔任研究助理，負責資料處理與統計，以及模型的搭建與操作。
這些教學資源目前已整理為開源教材並上傳至 GitHub，供學生與其他使用者取用。
我也熱衷推廣語言學在科技上的應用，曾參與「真的吧！訊息偵探事務所」專案，從語言分析出發拆解錯假訊息的書寫手法，並開發 LINE 機器人「討厭詭圖鑑」進行互動式展示，該專案於 2022 資訊月展出，吸引超過百位用戶加入。
目前亦將理論結合prompt engineering，透過 Gemini 模型生成回應，用戶可自行輸入資訊，機器人會分析其中應留意之處，作為警示系統提醒用戶。
過去的經驗中，讓我累積了跨領域整合的能力，作為一名跨領域的人，我喜歡將抽象的語言理論轉譯成具體的應用可能。程式語言冷靜理性，人類語言本身充滿迷霧與情感，而我希望透過不斷的研究及落地應用，讓自己成為那個在兩者之間架起橋樑的人。

I am Chang-Yu Tsai, holding an MA in Linguistics from National Chengchi University. 
My academic background equips me with interdisciplinary skills bridging linguistic theory and natural language processing (NLP). 
With a focus on the variation and structure of language, I am deeply intrigued by how to enable computers to process—or more boldly, to understand—human language.
My master’s thesis focused on the NER task of body part names from Taiwanese healthcare texts, a task involving a low-resource language. 
I independently handled every stage of the project, including data preprocessing, entity labelling, linguistic feature extraction, and model training. 
Building upon this work, I also developed a customised GPT for automatic annotation, exploring few-shot learning in low-resource contexts. 
To support Taiwanese NLP, I recently built a RESTful API for my NER model trained on native data, making it accessible for downstream applications.
To strengthen my technical foundation, I systematically studied machine learning, deep learning, and generative AI. 
As a member of the KnULP Lab, I served as both a research and teaching assistant. 
I designed and led lab sessions covering classification, sequence labelling, seq2seq models, and topic modelling, and contributed to data analysis in lab projects. 
The programming materials I developed are available as open-source resources on my GitHub.
Beyond academia, I actively collaborated with various institutions. 
I joined a team project that developed a LINE chatbot designed to reveal linguistic cues in misinformation. 
The project was showcased at the 2022 Taiwan IT Month and attracted over 100 users during the exhibition. 
Currently, this LINE chatbot delivers warning messages based on the input texts provided by users. 
It integrates linguistic theories into the prompts fed to the Gemini model in order to generate appropriate responses.
Through hands-on experiences, I step by step built up my cross-disciplinary capabilities. 
As a cross-disciplinary explorer, I find joy in interpreting abstract linguistic theories into real-world applications. 
Programming languages may speak in logic and structure, while human language is rich with ambiguity and emotion. 
I aspire to be the bridge that connects these two worlds.
